Spirited Away

The film follows Chihiro, a young girl who finds herself trapped in a mysterious world and must navigate challenges to save her parents and return to the real world.

Director

The main art director was Hayao Miyazaki. He's also taken part in other projects such as My Neighbor Totoro, Princess Mononoke, Howl's Moving Castle, ect

Inspirations

The very core of Spirited Away is centered around the folklore from Miyazaki’s home and uses the lessons and ideas from Japanese Shinto, Buddhism, and Japanese folklore to help support the themes of the film.

The movie

July 20, 2001

The story is about the adventures of a young ten-year-old girl named Chihiro as she wanders into the world of the gods and spirits. She is forced to work at a bathhouse following her parents being turned into pigs by the evil witch Yubaba. The film was made to please the ten-year-old daughter of Hayao Miyazaki's personal friend, director Seiji Okuda. Okuda's daughter even became the model for the film's protagonist, Chihiro. During the film's planning phase, Miyazaki gathered the daughters of Ghibli's staff in a mountain hut in Shinano Province to hold a training seminar. His experience led him to wanting to make a film for them, since he had never made a movie for girls at the age of 10.
